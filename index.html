Sustainable Development

Sustainable development is about meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet theirs. In 2015, world leaders adopted the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development and its 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) to end poverty, protect the planet, and tackle inequalities. These goals offer a practical and effective pathway to address the causes of violent conflict, human rights abuses, climate change, and environmental degradation, ensuring no one is left behind.
